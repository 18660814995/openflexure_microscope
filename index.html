Openflexure microscope

An open-source 3D printable microscope, complete with high-precision XYZ stage.

3D Printed Microscopes

This project has been going for a couple of years now, aiming to make a microscope that not only has high-magnification optics but also a really good mechanical stage. Development is ongoing, but it's quite well optimised already, with a study on its mechanical performance and a first stable release on DocuBricks. This site is a little sparse at present, but the repository now has all the source files for the microscope.

If you'd like to build one yourself, it's best to download a release rather than just heading to the STL folder in the repository, because there's no guarantee the STL files in there will be up to date and there will be a large number of duplicate parts. There is full documentation with lots of photos included in the zip download in DocuBricks format, but thanks to WaterScope and Redgate we also have a rewritten set of assembly instructions. Currently all the documentation and releases focus on the "basic" version of the microscope (i.e. a Raspberry Pi camera, using the lens from the camera module as the microscope objective). It's possible to put it together with other options, including a motorised stage and an RMS objective, and I'm working on documenting that.

Projects based on OpenFlexure Microscope

This design has been used in lots of different places, from labs to schools - but one initiative I'm very keen to support is WaterScope, a not for profit start-up aiming to make testing for bacterial contamination much faster, cheaper, and easier to use. The translation stage also found its way into an Optical Projection Tomography system built by a student team at Cambridge. It was initially funded through a Cambridge SynBioFund project, and has also been supported by the EPSRC.
